Why we are doing this
01

Outcome, not output

A KPI measures whether a direction was correct — not whether one feature shipped. Features are the means; the KPI is the end.

02

A filter for the roadmap

An agreed KPI lets us decline work that does not move the number. It is the reason to say no to ad-hoc requests.

03

Owned across teams

Product owns the number even when the infrastructure behind it sits on another team; engineering delivers the data. Instrumenting it is a shared commitment.

04

Rests on immutable data

Every KPI sits on a stable, self-observable data source so the definition doesn't silently drift as internals change. No KPI built on off-limits or unstable internals.
