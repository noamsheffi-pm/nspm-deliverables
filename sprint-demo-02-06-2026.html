Your Streaming Datasets, Now a First-Class Processing Target

Streaming Datasets — Enrichment, Templating & Multi-Priority Routing Follow-up

Last sprint you could carve your own streaming datasets under default/ and route TCO traffic into them. Now those datasets are a full processing target: apply enrichment and templating to data as it streams in, and route multiple priorities into a single target dataset — a streaming dataset now behaves like any first-class Coralogix destination.

↗ Roadmap card

Enrichment on Streaming Datasets

Enrichment rules now apply to data routed into user-defined streaming datasets — augment records on the way in, scoped per dataset.

Templating in Streaming Datasets

Reusable templates for routing and enrichment — configure a streaming dataset once and apply it consistently.

Multi-Priority per Target

Route more than one TCO priority into the same target dataset — a target is no longer limited to a single priority.

Quota-Based Routing — No More Cliffs

Gradual TCO Priority Management — Multi-Step Quota Routing

Define up to three priority tiers that automatically activate as your team's daily quota fills. Instead of all data dropping to Block at a single threshold, route the first 50% of quota as High, the next 30% as Medium, and only block as you approach the limit — fine-grained control over cost and availability without losing critical signals.

↗ Roadmap card

Up to 3 Priority Tiers Per Policy

High → Medium → Low → Block as quota fills. Block is terminal. Keep medium-value data flowing while shedding low-value first.

Configurable Default Priority

Prefilled from your system TCO default, fully overridable. The "everything else" tail is yours to set.

Pairs with the Preview Panel

DPXL TCO Preview shows every transition point on the chart, so you tune all N boundaries against real traffic.

Quota Transition Events & Alerting New

When a quota-based TCO policy crosses a threshold or its quota resets, Radiant now emits a system-dataset event — so you can alert on quota exhaustion, audit when & why data priority changed, and correlate transitions with cost spikes, all through standard DataPrime queries and alerts. Closes the observability gap 5 customers flagged (Via, Acko, FullPath, NielsenIQ, Happening).

↗ Roadmap card

Event on Every Transition

Each High → Medium → Block step — and the daily quota reset — emits a system-dataset event within 60s. Multi-tier cascades emit one event per step.

Queryable in DataPrime

Events land in a system dataset and query like any other data — no parallel path, no special tooling.

Standard Alerts on Exhaustion

Build normal Coralogix alerts on the transition events — know the moment a policy drops priority or starts blocking.

Audit & Correlate

See exactly when and why priority changed, and correlate quota exhaustion with cost spikes or data gaps. Fire-and-forget — emission never affects routing.

Quota Rules

Guarantee Capacity for Any entityType

Unit-Based Quota Reservations — Absolute Locks, Admin-Managed

Reserve a fixed number of units for any entityType within your quota — so critical workloads always have guaranteed capacity, no matter how the rest of your pipeline behaves. Unlike percentage-based rules that shift when plans change, unit locks hold their value absolutely. Admins can mark rules organization-managed, preventing users from overriding reserved capacity.

↗ Roadmap card

Absolute Unit Locks

Reserve a fixed unit count for any entityType. Unlike % rules that drift when the plan changes, a unit lock holds its value absolutely.

Guaranteed Capacity Under Pressure

Critical workloads keep their reserved units no matter how the rest of the pipeline behaves — no contention, no starvation.

Admin / Org-Managed Locks

Mark a rule organization-managed so users can't override reserved capacity. Enforcement is code-level, not policy-level.

Plan-Aware & Transfer-Safe

The system rejects reservations that exceed the plan, and org-team unit transfers respect locks — reserved units are excluded from what can be moved.
